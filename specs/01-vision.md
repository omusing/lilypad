# Vision

## What Lilypad is

A free, private pain tracking app for people living with chronic pain and the
caregivers of geriatric patients. It helps users log their pain twice daily —
morning and evening — and generates a clinical-grade report they can bring to
provider appointments.

The twice-daily cadence captures the two clinically meaningful anchors of the
day: how the patient woke up (overnight and morning state) and how the day
resolved (end-of-day state). These anchors reflect natural diurnal variation in
pain levels and are the data points providers most commonly ask about. The
cadence also coincides with the most prevalent chronic pain medication schedule
(BID), making the logging habit easy to layer onto an existing routine — but
the check-in is not tied to medication intake. A patient on a once-daily or
three-times-daily schedule still benefits from the same two check-in windows.

No account. No cloud. No data sent anywhere. Ever.

## Why it exists

The gap between how a patient experiences pain and how they describe it to a
provider in a 10-minute appointment costs people better care. Lilypad closes
that gap by making consistent logging easy and turning accumulated entries into
a report a provider can actually use in the room.

It was built by a licensed pain management nurse practitioner for their own
patient community. That clinical grounding — knowing which data points matter
in a real provider conversation — is what separates it from generic symptom
trackers.

## Who it's for

**Primary user:** A person aged 40–70+ dealing with chronic pain, or a caregiver
managing pain care for an elderly family member. They follow the influencer's
content and already trust their clinical guidance. They are not necessarily
tech-savvy. The app must work for someone who has never set up a health app before.

**Secondary user:** The healthcare provider who receives the generated report.
They are not a user of the app — they are a consumer of its output. The report
must be readable and clinically useful without any context about the app.

## What success looks like

- A user can complete a pain entry in under 60 seconds
- After 7 days of entries, a provider can look at the generated report and say
  "this tells me something I wouldn't have gotten from a 10-minute conversation"
- Users log consistently because the app fits into an existing habit (medication
  administration), not because they remember to open it
- Zero data incidents — no user data ever reaches any external server

## What it is NOT

- Not a diagnostic tool. Not medical advice. Lilypad records what the user
  reports; it does not interpret, recommend, or diagnose.
- Not a telehealth or provider-facing platform. Providers interact with a PDF,
  not the app.
- Not a subscription product. Free, permanently, for the influencer's audience.
- Not a general wellness app. The target user has real, diagnosed chronic pain.
  The design reflects that.
